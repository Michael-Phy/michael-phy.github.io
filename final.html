Three Project Options

1. Parkinson’s Anti-Tremor Tableware Helper

This arm-mounted device is designed to assist individuals with Parkinson's disease in maintaining independence during meals. It features a 3D-printed frame, soft foam padding, a contact sensor, a DC motor. The padding gently cradles the user's arm, minimizing involuntary hand movements while eating. A contact sensor continuously monitors grip strength; should it fall below a predetermined threshold, the motor activates to securely lock chopsticks or spoons in place, preventing them from slipping.

2. Wearable Reading Magnifier

This head-mounted device offers enhanced reading support for individuals with vision impairment. It incorporates optical lenses, light sensors, control buttons, LED lights, and adjustable hinge structures. The light sensors detect ambient light conditions, while control buttons allow users to easily adjust the LED lights' brightness. Flexible hinge structures enable precise positioning of the lenses to suit individual needs. This device is designed to make reading both paper books and digital screens more comfortable and accessible.

3. Posture Correction Wearable Device

Designed to promote better posture, this back-mounted device integrates position sensors, vibration motors, and indicator lights. The position sensors continuously track spinal alignment. If the monitored angles exceed predefined healthy limits, the sensors trigger the vibration motors. These motors then generate gentle vibration signals to discreetly alert the user to adjust their posture. This device aims to alleviate physical strain often associated with prolonged screen time and desk work.

My Preferred Project

Among these three proposals, I've chosen the Parkinson’s Anti-Tremor Tableware Helper. This device holds significant promise in enabling individuals with Parkinson's disease to manage their meal intake independently, fostering greater autonomy and dignity in their daily lives.
