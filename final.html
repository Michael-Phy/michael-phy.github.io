Three Project Options

1. Parkinson’s Anti-Tremor Tableware Helper

This arm-mounted tool contains a 3D printed frame, foam padding, contact sensor, DC motor, LED lights, and piezoelectric buzzer. Padding sits between the frame and user’s arm to limit hand displacement during eating. The contact sensor records grip force values from the user’s hand. When recorded values drop below a fixed threshold, the sensor sends signals to activate the motor. The motor locks chopsticks or spoons to the frame to stop sliding movement. Light sensors connect to LED lights. LED lights receive trigger signals and turn on when surrounding light levels drop below a set value. The buzzer outputs audio signals to deliver reminders to users. This project outputs a device for daily use. Construction uses entry-level manufacturing techniques. The device serves users with involuntary hand movement conditions to access assistive equipment for independent eating.

2. Wearable Reading Magnifier

This head-mounted device carries optical lenses, light sensors, control buttons, LED lights, and hinge structures. Light sensors record surrounding light levels. Buttons send signals to adjust power output for LED lights. Hinge structures control lens position angles. The device supports text reading from paper books and digital display screens for users with vision impairment.

3. Posture Correction Wearable Device

This back-mounted device integrates position sensors, vibration motors, and indicator lights. Position sensors track spinal angle data. When tracked angles exceed preset range limits, sensors activate vibration motors. Vibration motors generate vibration signals to notify users. The device reduces physical strain from long periods of screen viewing and desk study.

My Preferred Project

I select the Parkinson’s Anti-Tremor Tableware Helper among the three proposals. The device supports users with Parkinson’s disease to complete meal intake without assistance from other people.
