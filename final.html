Three Project Options

1. Parkinson’s Anti-Tremor Tableware Helper

This arm tool has a printed frame, padding, hand sensor, motor, lights, and buzzer. Padding limits hand shake during meals. The sensor activates the motor if the user cannot hold utensils. The motor holds chopsticks or spoons to stop sliding. Lights turn on in dark spaces. The buzzer sends alert sounds. This project produces a low-cost daily tool. The build uses basic production methods for people with hand movement disorders to get assistive equipment.

2. Wearable Reading Magnifier

This head device supports reading books and screens for people with vision loss. It includes adjustable lights and lens angles.

3. Posture Correction Wearable Device

This back device prompts users to maintain sitting posture. It reduces body problems from screen time and study.

My Preferred Project

I choose the Parkinson’s Anti-Tremor Tableware Helper from the three ideas. The tool lets people with Parkinson’s complete meals without support.
