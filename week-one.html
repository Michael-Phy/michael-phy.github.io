Weekly Lab Notes & Work

This week, I successfully set up my personal course portfolio website and completed basic webpage configuration and navigation layout. The main task was brainstorming and proposing three feasible final project ideas based on real-life experiences.

The first project is a Parkinson’s anti-tremor tableware helper. This idea is inspired by my father, who suffers from Parkinson’s disease. I have observed his daily struggles with uncontrollable hand tremors while eating, which makes independent dining difficult. This wearable assistive device is designed to stabilize tableware and help patients eat more independently.

The second and third projects originate from my own poor study habits. As a long-term desk student, I often maintain incorrect sitting postures and lean too close to books when studying. These habits have caused poor posture problems and severe myopia. Therefore, I proposed a wearable posture correction device that provides vibration reminders to fix slouched sitting posture, and an adjustable head-mounted eye protection magnifier to standardize reading distance and reduce eye strain during long study hours.

After comparing the practical value, feasibility, and social significance of the three ideas, I finally selected the Parkinson’s anti-tremor tableware helper as my official final project.
